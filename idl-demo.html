how to play

tempo is a listening game played by ear

Your goal is to guess the BPM of a beat by ear. Listen first, then use the slider to match the speed you remember.

  1. Press start. A round begins with a short beat sample. Do not worry about numbers yet; just listen for how fast it feels.
  2. Wait for the beat to stop. When the listening part ends, the slider unlocks and your starting guess appears.
  3. Move the slider. Drag left if the beat felt slower, or right if it felt faster. In normal mode, your lamp previews the BPM you are guessing.
  4. Lock in. When the speed feels right, press lock in. You will see the real BPM, your guess, how far off you were, and your points.
  5. Compare the lamps. In normal and endless modes, orange shows the real beat and green shows your guess. In hard mode, only the real beat is replayed.
  6. Finish the run. Normal and hard last five rounds. Endless continues until a guess misses the current limit. Copy your result to share it.

normal mode

You hear 8 ticks, then you can preview your guess while moving the slider. This is the recommended way to learn the game.

hard mode

You hear only 4 ticks, and there is no preview while guessing. Hard mode is for when you want the guess to be fully from memory.

endless mode

Endless plays like normal mode, but the run continues only while your guess stays within the limit. The limit tightens from 8% to 5% as you clear more rounds.

scoring

Closer guesses earn more points. A perfect round is 100 points. Five-round modes top out at 500, while an endless total grows with the run.
